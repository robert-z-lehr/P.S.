Civil Engineering Probability and Statistics Reference

Concept/Term/Jargon Definition Use Case / Example Relevant Formula(s) Relevant Probability Distribution(s) Sub-Domain in Civil Engineering
Union of Events The probability of either of two events occurring Estimating the probability of either of two structural failures $$ P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) $$ Binomial, Poisson, Normal Structural Engineering
Intersection of Events The probability of both events occurring simultaneously Calculating the joint probability of both load and material failures $$ P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B) $$ Binomial, Poisson Structural Engineering
Conditional Probability The probability of an event given that another event has occurred Determining the probability of delays given adverse weather $$ P(A\|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} $$ Binomial, Poisson, Normal Transportation Engineering
Bayes' Theorem A way to update the probability of an event based on new evidence Updating the probability of construction site safety violations based on new evidence $$ P(A\|B) = \frac{P(B\|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} $$ Binomial, Poisson, Normal Risk Analysis
Total Probability Rule The total probability of an event which can be partitioned into a set of disjoint events Assessing overall failure probabilities from multiple independent sources $$ P(B) = \sum_{i} P(B\|A_i) \cdot P(A_i) $$ Binomial, Poisson, Normal Structural Engineering
Complement Rule The probability of the complement of an event is one minus the probability of the event Finding the probability that a material does not fail within its expected lifespan $$ P(A') = 1 - P(A) $$ Any Reliability Engineering
Expectation (Mean) The expected value or mean of a random variable Calculating the average load on a bridge over time $$ E(X) = \sum x \cdot P(X = x) $$ Normal, Binomial Structural Engineering
Variance The measure of the dispersion of a set of values Measuring variability in material strength $$ \sigma^2 = E[(X - \mu)^2] = \sum (x - \mu)^2 \cdot P(X = x) $$ Normal, Binomial, Poisson Material Science
Standard Deviation The square root of the variance, indicating the spread of a set of values Assessing the spread in concrete compressive strength data $$ \sigma = \sqrt{\sigma^2} $$ Normal Material Science
Binomial Distribution Probability distribution summarizing the number of successes in a fixed number of trials Determining the probability of a certain number of defective items in a batch $$ P(X = k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} $$ Binomial Quality Control, Manufacturing
Poisson Distribution Probability distribution describing the number of events occurring in a fixed interval of time or space Modeling the number of vehicle arrivals at a toll booth $$ P(X = k) = \frac{\lambda^k e^{-\lambda}}{k!} $$ Poisson Traffic Engineering
Exponential Distribution Probability distribution describing the time between events in a Poisson process Estimating the time between successive failures of a component $$ f(x; \lambda) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x} $$ Exponential Reliability Engineering
Normal Distribution Probability distribution that is symmetric about the mean, showing that data near the mean are more frequent Modeling measurement errors in survey data $$ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}} e^{-\frac{(x - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}} $$ Normal Surveying, Geotechnical Engineering
Uniform Distribution Probability distribution where all outcomes are equally likely Simulating random sampling in material testing $$ f(x) = \frac{1}{b - a} \text{ for } a \leq x \leq b $$ Uniform Material Science
Geometric Distribution Probability distribution of the number of Bernoulli trials needed to get one success Modeling the number of inspections needed to find the first defect $$ P(X = k) = (1-p)^{k-1} p $$ Geometric Quality Control, Inspection
Negative Binomial Distribution Probability distribution of the number of trials needed to achieve a specified number of successes Estimating the number of inspections required to find a specified number of defects $$ P(X = k) = \binom{k + r - 1}{r - 1} (1 - p)^r p^k $$ Negative Binomial Quality Control, Inspection
Weibull Distribution Probability distribution used to model the time to failure of materials and components Modeling the time to failure of materials and components $$ f(x; \lambda, k) = \frac{k}{\lambda} \left( \frac{x}{\lambda} \right)^{k-1} e^{-\left( \frac{x}{\lambda} \right)^k} $$ Weibull Reliability Engineering, Material Science
Lognormal Distribution Probability distribution of a random variable whose logarithm is normally distributed Modeling particle sizes in granular materials $$ f(x; \mu, \sigma) = \frac{1}{x \sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{(\ln x - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}} $$ Lognormal Geotechnical Engineering
Beta Distribution Probability distribution defined on the interval [0, 1], often used in Bayesian statistics Modeling strength proportions of composite materials $$ f(x; \alpha, \beta) = \frac{x^{\alpha-1} (1-x)^{\beta-1}}{B(\alpha, \beta)} $$ Beta Material Science
Triangular Distribution Probability distribution with a single peak, used for estimating project completion times Estimating project completion times with known minimum, maximum, and most likely durations $$ f(x) = \begin{cases} \frac{2(x - a)}{(b - a)(c - a)} & \text{for } a \leq x \leq c \\ \frac{2(b - x)}{(b - a)(b - c)} & \text{for } c \leq x \leq b \end{cases} $$ Triangular Project Management
Pareto Distribution Probability distribution used to describe the distribution of wealth, sizes of cities, etc. Modeling distribution of extreme loads in structural engineering $$ f(x; \alpha, x_m) = \frac{\alpha x_m^\alpha}{x^{\alpha + 1}} \text{ for } x \geq x_m $$ Pareto Structural Engineering
Gumbel Distribution Probability distribution used to model the distribution of the maximum (or the minimum) of a number of samples Modeling the distribution of extreme values such as maximum wind speeds $$ f(x; \mu, \beta) = \frac{1}{\beta} e^{-(z + e^{-z})} \text{ where } z = \frac{x - \mu}{\beta} $$ Gumbel Environmental Engineering, Risk Analysis
Frechet Distribution Probability distribution used to model extreme values Modeling extreme values in hydrology $$ f(x; \alpha) = \alpha \left( \frac{x_m}{x} \right)^{\alpha + 1} e^{-\left( \frac{x_m}{x} \right)^\alpha} \text{ for } x \geq x_m $$ Frechet Hydrological Engineering
Rayleigh Distribution Probability distribution used for random variables that are non-negative Modeling wave heights and wind speeds $$ f(x; \sigma) = \frac{x}{\sigma^2} e^{-\frac{x^2}{2\sigma^2}} \text{ for } x \geq 0 $$ Rayleigh Coastal and Wind Engineering
